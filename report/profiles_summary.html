Key cross-cutting findings: Water shortage affects all strata equally — ~42% of households ran out of water in the past month (Informal 42.3%, Hybrid 42.0%, Formal 42.9%): piped access does not protect from intermittent supply. Heat at home is the top-ranked urban health priority across all three strata (73–91% of respondents), reflecting a shared exposure that transcends the socioeconomic gradient.
Overall profile — 10 dimensions  (all expressed as % · shaded band = 95% CI)

Basic sanitation = access to improved toilet + basic hygiene facility  ·  Basic hygiene = soap + running water  ·  Not overcrowded = ≤ 2 persons/room  ·  Reliable water supply = did not run out in past month  ·  HCP consulted = past 12 months  ·  Long-term residence = ≥10 years at current address (incl. always lived here)  ·  Perception of good health and climate change awareness from individual sub-sample (n ≈ 626–708)

Housing & WASH
Water access paradox: Piped water (SODECI) coverage ranges from 83% (Informal) to 96% (Formal), yet reliable supply is similar across strata (57% / 58% / 57%) — piped access does not protect from intermittent supply. Basic sanitation gap: 43% (Informal) vs 82% (Formal).
Healthcare access
Insurance gap: Valid health insurance coverage ranges from 8.9% (Informal) to 17.6% (Hybrid) to 29.0% (Formal) — a >3× gap between the extremes. Financial barriers to care: 30% (Informal) vs 19% (Formal).
Wellbeing & health outcomes (sub-sample)
Mental health burden: PHQ-9 moderate–severe symptoms (score ≥ 8) affect 8.1% (Informal) and 8.7% (Hybrid) vs 4.4% (Formal) — roughly 2× higher burden in less formal strata. Climate change awareness also tracks the socioeconomic gradient: 43% → 63% → 80%.
Urban health priorities (% selecting · multi-select question)
Heat dominates across all strata (73% / 91% / 87%). Sanitation and road conditions are disproportionately cited by Informal residents (44% / 43% vs 18% / 12% in Formal), reflecting unmet basic infrastructure needs. Insecurity is most prominent in Hybrid (20%).
Age-Friendly Environments — 10 dimensions  (1–5 scale · lower = better · closer to centre = more age-friendly · shaded band = 95% CI)

Items rated 1–5 · lower = better (score 1 = most age-friendly, score 5 = least age-friendly). Closer to centre = more age-friendly environment. Aged 50+: n = 454 (Informal 151 · Hybrid 161 · Formal 142). Aged 60+: n = 221 (Informal 65 · Hybrid 80 · Formal 76).

Built environment & urban morphology — 7 dimensions  (remote-sensing / building-footprint metrics · per-cluster then stratum mean)

Two-stage aggregation: each metric is computed per survey cluster (32 grappes, using the cluster polygon), then averaged across the 10–11 clusters of each stratum (equal weight per cluster). Radar axes are min-max scaled to the observed cluster range (0 = lowest, 1 = highest cluster); shaded band = 95% CI of the stratum mean across its clusters. Actual mean values are tabulated on the Descriptive Report page. Informal = densest / least green / lowest night-time lighting; Formal = greenest / least built-up; Hybrid = most grid-like streets (highest meshedness). Night-time lights (VIIRS, ~463 m) are a relative urban-infrastructure proxy.

Overall profile — 10 dimensions  (all expressed as % · shaded band = 95% CI)

Basic sanitation = access to improved toilet + basic hygiene facility  ·  Basic hygiene = soap + running water  ·  Not overcrowded = ≤ 2 persons/room  ·  Reliable water supply = did not run out in past month  ·  HCP consulted = past 12 months  ·  Long-term residence = ≥10 years at current address (incl. always lived here)  ·  Perception of good health and climate change awareness from individual sub-sample (n = 647–678 per stratum)

Housing & WASH
Water access paradox: Piped water (SODECI) coverage ranges from 82% (Informal) to 96% (Formal), yet reliable supply is similar across strata (58% / 57% / 57%) — piped access does not protect from intermittent supply. Basic sanitation gap: 41% (Informal) vs 82% (Formal).
Healthcare access
Insurance gap: Valid health insurance coverage ranges from 7.0% (Informal) to 18.7% (Hybrid) to 29.0% (Formal) — a ≈4× gap between the extremes. Financial barriers to care: 30% (Informal) vs 19% (Formal).
Wellbeing & health outcomes (sub-sample)
Mental health burden: PHQ-9 moderate–severe symptoms (score ≥ 8) affect 7.6% (Informal) and 9.2% (Hybrid) vs 4.4% (Formal) — roughly 1.9× the Formal burden in the less formal strata. Climate change awareness also tracks the socioeconomic gradient: 40% → 65% → 80%.
Urban health priorities (% selecting · multi-select question)
Heat dominates across all strata (79% / 84% / 87%). Sanitation and road conditions are disproportionately cited by Informal residents (47% / 47% vs 18% / 12% in Formal), reflecting unmet basic infrastructure needs. Insecurity is most prominent in Hybrid (19%).
Age-Friendly Environments — 10 dimensions  (scores inverted: 6 − raw · higher = better · further from centre = more age-friendly · shaded band = 95% CI)

Questionnaire items rated 1–5 (1 = strongly agree = most age-friendly). Built environment & urban morphology — 7 dimensions  (remote-sensing / building-footprint metrics · per-cluster then stratum mean)

Two-stage aggregation: each metric is computed per survey cluster (32 grappes, using the cluster polygon), then averaged across the 10–12 clusters of each stratum (equal weight per cluster). Radar axes are min-max scaled to the observed cluster range (0 = lowest, 1 = highest cluster); shaded band = 95% CI of the stratum mean across its clusters. Actual mean values are tabulated on the Descriptive Report page. Informal = densest / least green / lowest night-time lighting; Formal = greenest / least built-up; most grid-like streets (highest meshedness): Formal. Night-time lights (VIIRS, ~463 m) are a relative urban-infrastructure proxy.
